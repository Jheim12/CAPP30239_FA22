Do Railroads Need to Become more Flexible to Demand?

What are Flexible Schedules?

Flexible schedules enabled by AI could have a large potential (reduce emissions, save taxpayer money, etc.).
However, emphasis in on infrastructure with the latest schedule innovation being three decades old. (Gotthard, FV dosto (some of which are a disaster))
Therefore, it is worth taking a look at this cheap and fast to implement source of innovation. Let's take a look at why infrastructure alone is not all that great.

Infrastructure isn't King of Currywurst

Infrastructure in Germany is better than in Switzerland.
However, it is perceived as worse because of delays (operations).
Therefore, if you don't know how to run trains, infrastructure won't save you.

An Economist's Perspective

Economist would quantify the demand for rail transport services with Pkm. Switzerland is an outlier, which confirms the good infrastructure and operations.
However, all countries suffered during diminishing demand during the pandemic.
Therefore, the economist would expect a lower offer, meaning lower Tkm.

Did Adam Smith Prevail?

Tkm remained constant while Pkm plummeted. Discussion on public good.
However, that has poor consequences on environment and taxpayer money.
Therefore, flexible schedules could be helpful if we sacrifice some long-term planning. When the the pros of flexible schedules outweigh the cons?
